// About Me

From inventor at 9 to robotics engineer.

I've been fascinated by solving problems for as long as I can remember. At age nine in Malawi, I invented a solution that made soap float on water — it got an official patent and an little nice award from my school. That moment crystallised something: I wanted to be someone who creates things that didn't exist before.

In my undergraduate degree in Mechatronic Engineering at Stellenbosch University in South Africa, I learned how to design and build robots. Now I'm completing my Master's in Mobile and Autonomous Robotic Systems at Grenoble INP – Ense3 in France, where I'm learning how to make them intelligent — specialising in AI, navigation, perception, and control.

Currently, I'm a research intern at GIPSA-Lab working on Physics-Informed Neural Networks and reinforcement learning for aerial robotics, and I lead a team building an autonomous medical delivery drone for the European Robotics League competition.
